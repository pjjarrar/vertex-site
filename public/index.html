Concrete Scanning

Concrete scanning is essential for locating components that are critical in nature to a structure’s integrity.

When your project requires demolition of any type, it is critical to know the location of post-tension cables, conduits and structural reinforcing. Having this informationn in real-time ensures safer working conditions and greater strike reduction.

FAQ

Frequently asked Questions

Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR) often referred to as Concrete Scanning or Concrete X-ray, works by sending a pulse of energy into concrete and measuring the strength and time of the returning signal. The equipment will repeat this action multiple times as the unit is maneuvered across a surface and process the returning data points. This processed data will then appear on the operating system’s monitor and is interpreted by the GPR Operator.

Findings are collected in real time. This means there is no post job processing that needs to be completed. A GPR Operator can mark out findings as they go along, allowing for timely decisions.

Once an area is scanned the findings will be marked directly on the surface. This can be done with spray paint, or lumber crayon. If items can be categorized they will be color coded and labeled. Reports of findings can also be created.

Yes, GPR is completely safe to operate around people. The frequency emissions are less than that of a smart phone.

The equipment is very compact and mobile. Most surfaces can be scanned as long as the area is relatively smooth and dry.

When scanning concrete, GPR has an accuracy of approximately +/- a quarter of an inch horizontally from the center of the item and one-half inch vertically from the surface of the scan to the top of the item.

No, GPR is very quiet to operate.

Subsurface Utility Locating

Vertex GPR offers subsurface utility locating services utilizing Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR) and Electromagnetic Detection (EM). Subsurface utility locating is highly important for soil boring, excavation, directional boring, day-lighting, As-Built confirmation, and retrofit design.

Subsurface Utilities To Consider Prior To The Start of Your Project: Electrical, Gas, Communications, Sanitary Sewer, Storm Drains, Steam, Potable Water, Fire Services, and Irrigation.

UBICACIÓN DE SERVICIOS SUBTERRÁNEOS

Vertex GPR ofrece servicios para la ubicación de servicios subterráneos mediante la utilización de un radar de penetración de suelo (GPR) y detección electromagnética (EM). La ubicación de servicios subterráneos es muy importante para la perforación de suelo, la excavación, la perforación direccional, la iluminación natural, la conformidad de la obra terminada y el diseño actualizado.

Servicios Subterráneos Para Considerar Antes de Comenzar Su Proyecto:
Eléctrico, gas, comunicaciones, alcantarillado, desagüe pluvial, vapor, agua potable, servicios contra incendios e irrigación.

ESCANEO DE CONCRETO

A la hora de decidir dónde se va a taladrar o hacer cortes, nuestro servicio de escaneo de concreto proporciona información crucial. Identificando los peligros potenciales en tiempo real crea condiciones de trabajo más seguras, reduce el tiempo perdido, los excesos de costos, y ayuda en el diseño del proyecto.

¿Qué podemos ubicar?
Cables postensados, conductos incrustados, grosor del concreto, varilla, malla de alambre y formación de alvéolos/espacios vacíos.
